Feautures

We all have in mind what a high-end car is... or so we think because there are many who wonder what a high-end car is, what requirements it must meet and how we can distinguish a high-end car from one that it is not. It is, even though it belongs to the same firm. With the experience that years bring us with the sale of high-end cars from the highest-performance premium firms, we want to tell you the keys to knowing what a high-end car is and how to distinguish it.

  • Pointer and iconic design.

    If a high-end car does not have a valued and identifiable design, as is the case with Porsche, BMW, Ferrari or Mercdedes-Benz, it cannot be considered in this segment. The brands we are talking about seek with their designs to take their customers to another level, to another lifestyle. The design makes them stand out from the competition and, when that car passes, people turn to look at it.

  • Exclusive equipment

    Surely you know, or at least you know, Bang & Olufsen, Burmester, Bower & Wilkins or Naim, they are creators of unique and exclusive sound systems for certain brands of high-end cars, not present in general brands. Induction chargers for mobile phones integrated into the car, high-resolution touch screens, adaptive LED lighting, personalized seats adapted to the client, in short, equipment that can only be found in a high-end car.

  • Premium finishes with exclusive materials.

    In high-end cars, as in people, beauty is on the inside and it is this that gives these cars the name premium. It can have an innovative and recognizable design but inside it can be pure plastic and medium finishes that will lower that category car.

  • High-end exterior finishes.

    Carbon, metallic or matt paint, refined lines, iconic led or xenon headlights, unusual wheels or sporty exterior packages are typical of a high-end car.

  • High-end interior finishes.

    Carbon fiber dashboards, lava stone, compacted hemp, exclusive matte or glossy hand-carved woods, custom hand-treated premium leather, heated and massage-mode seats for driver and front passenger, laser etching, lighting packages unique, sound-insulating windowsâ€¦ Does anyone doubt that this is a high-end car?

Everything has a price.

Exclusivity is expensive, we know, but we must bear in mind that we pay for cutting-edge design, exclusive equipment and spectacular and well-studied finishes. The interesting thing is that the second-hand market offers you the possibility of accessing a high-end vehicle, with exclusive equipment and superior finishes, for much less than what a new one with fewer extras would cost.
